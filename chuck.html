20 Real Facts About Chuck Norris

#Fact: Chuck Norris was the first westerner to ever receive an 8th-degree black belt Grand Master in Taekwondo.

Chuck Norris is best known for his martial arts moves and consistent appearance on our television screens.

Have you ever wondered where he learned his martial arts skills or how he became so famous?

Here are 100 Chuck Norris facts that will answer some of the mysteries of this crazy character.

  1. Chuck Norris’s full name is Carlos Ray Norris. He is 82 years old and was born on March 10, 1940, in Ryan, Oklahoma, US.

  2. Norris’ father, Ray Dee Norris, was a World War II soldier and a man of many talents. Sadly he was also an alcoholic.

  3. Norris was named after his father’s minister, Carlos Berry.

  4. Norris has two younger brothers, Wieland and Aaron. Aaron is a Hollywood producer.

  5. He was an introvert at school; Norris was not athletic and was academically mediocre.

  6. Norris has shared that his parent’s unstable finances and the embarrassment of his father’s alcoholism are the main reasons for his introverted character as a child.

  7. His parents divorced when Norris was 16, which resulted in him moving with his mother and brothers to Torrance in California.

  8. Norris is best known for his acting, but he is a man of many talents. He is a martial artist, film producer, screenwriter, and he was an air policeman for the US Air Force.

  9. Norris married his high school sweetheart Dianne Kay Holechek. He was age 18, and she was 17 years old.

  10. In 1958 Norris became an Air Policeman for the United States Air Force. He was posted to Osan Air Base in South Korea.

  11. It was at this airbase that he acquired the nickname Chuck.

  12. While training in Osan, Norris began to learn the martial art of Tang Soo Do.

  13. He was discharged from the US Air Force in August 1962.

  14. Norris moved back to Torrance, California, where he applied to be a police officer.

  15. While on the waiting list for the police force, in 1962, Norris opened his first martial arts studio.

  16. To help promote his martial arts studio, Norris attended martial arts events and even did demonstrations and fights.

  17. Norris created his own form of martial art called Chun Kuk Do, which means Universal Way.

  18. Norris created the ten rules known as the code of honor for his martial art of Chun Kuk Do.

  19. The Chun Kuk Do world championship tournament is held every summer in Las Vegas, Nevada.

  20. Norris and Holechek had their first child, Mike, in 1962 and their second, Eric, in 1964. In 1963 Norris’ daughter from an affair was born.

#Fact: Chuck Norris is a man of many talents who has become one of the world’s most famous actors.

His contributions to the martial arts world have led him to become a legend and a founding member of the sport.

Norris’s energy and commitment to martial arts have inspired generations and are still being talked about today!
