20 Fun Facts About Dinosaurs That’ll Blow Your Mind

#Fact: Did you know that the largest T-Rex skeleton ever discovered was named Sue?

Dinosaurs have become some of the most out-there animals ever to have lived on this planet.

While it’s fun to imagine a world where dinosaurs and humans are alive during the same period, it’s probably not the best idea – we’ve all seen how it goes in Jurassic Park!

The next best thing is to learn all we can about these ancient reptiles and sit back and let our imagination run wild.

We’ve decided to give you a helping hand and have researched & written the best 20 dinosaur facts that you can imagine.

  1. Dinosaurs have been around for more than 230 million years!

  2. Dinosaurs lived during the Mesozoic era.

  3. They evolved from reptiles the size of house cats.

  4. Dinosaurs are still alive and with us today!All the non-avian dinosaurs went extinct, but the avian dinosaurs evolved over the millennia into birds.

  5. Pterodactyls, along with all other winged dinosaur-like reptiles, belong not to the dinosaur family but are classified as Pterosaurs.

  6. Dinosaurs are split into two main categories.To put it simply, either a dinosaur is a saurischian (Greek for “lizard-hipped”), or they’re an ornithischian (Greek for “bird-hipped”). Funnily enough, the lizard-hipped dinosaurs are more commonly related to modern-day birds, while the bird-hipped dinosaurs all went extinct!

  7. The first dinosaur was named “Megalosaurus” before we even knew dinosaurs existed.

  8. Just seven years after Buckland discovered the megalosaurus, a geologist and his wife came upon a new iguana-like skeleton in Sussex, England, which they named “Iguanadon.”

  9. When Sir Richard Owen came up with the name for dinosaurs, he meant the word terrible in a different sense

  10. When the iguanodon was first reconstructed, its thumb was placed on top of its nose.

  11. The megalosaurus was so popular in the 19th century that Charles Dickens included it in one of his novels.

  12. You can tell the difference between a dinosaur fossil and a stone by licking it. If you lick a suspected fossil, it will stick ever so slightly to your tongue because fossils are more porous than stones.

  13. More than 900 different dinosaurs roamed Earth.

  14. When dinosaurs first came onto the scene in the Triassic period, some 230 million years ago, the Earth’s continents were clustered together into one giant supercontinent called Pangaea.

  15. The largest dinosaurs were the titanosaurs

  16. The smallest sauropod was only slightly heavier than a bull.

  17. Gallimimuses had beaks instead of teeth.

  18. Most of the world’s dinosaur fossils are found in three places.

  19. Some pterosaurs were covered in fur. They would have appeared similar in many ways to a fruit bat, but at a much, much larger scale!

  20. Most fossilized dinosaur bones aren’t actually bones anymore.

#Fact: Blue whales can weigh up to 190 tons (172.4 tonnes) and grow 110 feet (34 me) long. The largest dinosaurs weigh up to 69 tons (62.6 tonnes) and measure up to 120 feet (36.5 m) long.

In general, whales can grow much heavier as their weight is supported by water, while dinosaurs still had to support their weight with their legs!

The craziest thing about dinosaurs is that there’s so much more that we don’t know about them!
